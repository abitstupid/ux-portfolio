Process

User Personas

I created a total of 7-8 initial personas. And then merged them into 3 final personas. Two of which are shown below.

Problems

Studies suggest that attention span of humans is continuosly decreasing. Which means normally people won't want to read long long articles. And ofcourse the frustration of reading the whole article and finding it non-informative makes the user leave the website. Also the ginormous continously increasing use of mobile phones means the platform must be "responsive" and "interactive."

Pain Points

Short attention span of current generation makes them skip long articles.

The frustration of reading the whole article and finding it non-informative.

Can’t verify the Fake News.

Goals

To find a way so that user can choose between short and long version of the news or article.

To let the user know if the article is worth reading or is informative before the user reads the article.

A way for users to report fake news and see if a news is fake or real.


Go to prototype section to know how these pain points and goals were met for better user experience.

Information Architecture

After the goals were converted into Ideas using Scenarios and Ideation process. The next thing was to define the information architecture. To define the architecture the first thing I did was to wrote down- all the content, solutions we created and information to be presented on the website, in a word document to get more familiarised with the content. Then at first I divided the content by "Subject". For Example : "Reading News and Articles in one subject" and "Writing News and Articles into another" and "Profile and Setttings in another." Then I did card sorting using Users to know how they divide the content. And mixed best of the both worlds to get this site map shown below the initial rough sketches.
